    Description

    Control your hearing aids discreetly with the touch of a finger


    This app lets you control your hearing aids from your mobile device. It comes with an app for Apple Watch helping you discreetly change programs and adjust volume (when within Bluetooth range).


    Just a note: some features may be available depending on your hearing aid model. Check below for details.


    • Adjust the sound volume for each hearing aid together or separately

    • Mute surroundings for better focus

    • Switch between programs set by your hearing care professional

    • Check battery levels

    • Use SpeechBooster to reduce background noise and enhance speech (available for all hearing aid models except Oticon Opn™)

    • Stream calls, music, and podcasts directly to your hearing aids (availability may vary depending on your phone model)

    • Find your hearing aids if lost (requires location services to always be turned on)

    • Access app support and troubleshooting solutions

    • Meet your hearing care professional for an online visit (by appointment)

    • Adjust streaming sounds with streaming equalizer (available for all hearing aid models except Oticon Opn™ and Oticon Siya)

    • Adjust sounds around you with sound equalizer (available for Oticon Intent™ and Oticon Real™ models)

    • Track your progress with HearingFitness™ feature (available for Oticon Intent™ and Oticon Real™ models)

    • Handle wireless accessories paired with your hearing aids such as TV Adapters, Oticon EduMic or ConnectClip


    First use:

    You need to pair your hearing aids with this app to use it to control your hearing aids.


    App availability:

    The app is compatible with most hearing aid models. If you have hearing aids from 2016-2018 and haven't updated them yet, a hearing aid update is required for this app to work. We recommend regular hearing aid updates during your routine check-up with your hearing care professional.


    To check the latest list of compatible devices, please visit:

    https://www.oticon.com/support/compatibility

    Developer Response ,

    Hi, thank you for your feedback. I am very sorry to hear that you are having these in-app disconnections. We will be happy to do all we can to help get you a stable connection. Please write us at appsupport@oticon.com and we will be happy to do all we can to troubleshoot this so that you can have a stable connection between your hearing aids and iPhone. A common cause for in-app disconnections can be if the hearing aids are connected to another device, such as an iPad. If this is the case, the way to transfer the connection between devices running iOS 17 is to change the audio input by tapping the airplay symbol in the top right part of the control center (opened by swiping down from the top right corner of your device) and choosing your hearing aids. For devices running iOS 16 or older you can transfer the in-app connection by starting to play any sound from the device you want to use with your hearing aids. Alternatively, it can be necessary in some cases to toggle Bluetooth OFF on the iOS device not used with the hearing aids. Best regards, Oticon
